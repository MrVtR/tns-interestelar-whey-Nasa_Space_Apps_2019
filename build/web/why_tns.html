Plants in space!
  Imagine passing through the wonders of the cosmos with a green friend to bring joy into the trip. Did you? Well, all of that is possible with Trans-Neptunian Space way. Our ship has the necessary accommodations for a greenhouse onboard the ship. It is composed of various small sized gardens (approximately 6 plants per gardens), each one filled with clay-based structure and fertilizers for the plant to grow. Besides, each garden has bank light emitting diodes (LEDs) that produce a spectrum of light to orient the plant’s growth.

Spaceship

When it comes to the machine that makes this trip possible, we have the best option! The spacecraft will be an enhanced version of the Falcon Heavy operation rocket, designed by SpaceX. It will be able to carry large payloads such as crew, passengers, luggage and fuel. The Falcon Heavy will also carry the Dragon spacecraft, which is capable of carrying up to 7 passengers. It consists in a free-flying spacecraft designed for orbiting destinations and beyond. The propulsion of the Falcon heavy will done by EM drive(Electromagnetic engine), with the help of gravitational pull of planets. This works the same way as a slingshot, sending the rocket away! The Dragon spacecraft contain Draco thrusters, which allow orbital maneuvering.

Communication

Contact will be made by radio waves - a type of electromagnetics wave - that travels in the speed of light. We will use the same system that Curiosity(Rover) had back in 2012, in NASA's Mars Science Laboratory mission. It is composed by three differents antennas coupled to the Sounder. The first one has low potency, the second has high potency, and the last has high speed and is connected with a radio UHF (Ultra High Frequency).

Heat and Cold

Some peoples say "In this trip the spaceship won't be freezing??" the answer to this question is "NO!".
We will use the same ISS' system, the MLI (Multi-Layer Insulation) that consists in protecting the spaceship to sun radiation, and prevent that the spacecraft loses heat by radiation. We will also use the Active Thermal Control System, to regulate the internal temperature.
